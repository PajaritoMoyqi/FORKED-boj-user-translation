Checksum

Statement

KOI Electronics, a computer manufacturing company, assigns a unique 6-digit number to each computer it manufactures. The first 5 digits of the unique number can be any number from 00000 to 99999, and the 6th digit is the checksum. The checksum is the remainder when the sum of the squares of the first 5 digits is divided by 10.

For example, if the first 5 digits of the unique number are 04256, then the sum of the squares of these numbers is $0 + 16 + 4 + 25 + 36 = 81$. The remainder when 81 is divided by 10 is 1, which is the checksum.

Input

The first line provides the first 5 digits of the unique number, each separated by a space.

Output

Print the checksum on the first line.
