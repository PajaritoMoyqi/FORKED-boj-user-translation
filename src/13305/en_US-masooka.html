Gas Station

Statement

There are $N$ cities in a certain country, all located along a straight horizontal road for convenience. One plans to travel by car from the city on the far left to the city on the far right. The roads between adjacent cities may have different lengths, with the unit of length being kilometers (km).

When starting the journey, the car has no fuel, so it is necessary to fill up at a gas station. The fuel tank size is unlimited, allowing one to fill as much fuel as needed. The car uses 1 liter of fuel per kilometer traveled. Each city has only one gas station, and the price per liter of fuel can vary from city to city, with the unit of price being dollars.

For example, consider a country with four cities arranged as in the following figure. The number inside the circle is the price per liter at the gas station in that city. The numbers on the road represent the length of the road.

If you fill up with 6 liters of fuel in the far-left city and travel to the far-right city without further refueling, the total cost is 30 dollars. However, if you fill up with 2 liters of fuel in the far-left city ($2 \times 5 = 10$ dollars), travel to the next city, fill up with 3 liters ($3 \times 2 = 6$ dollars), then fill up with another liter in the next city ($1 \times 4 = 4$ dollars) before traveling to the far-right city, the total cost is 20 dollars. Another method is to fill up with 2 liters in the far-left city ($2 \times 5 = 10$ dollars), travel to the next city, fill up with 4 liters ($4 \times 2 = 8$ dollars), and then travel to the far-right city, the total cost is 18 dollars.

Write a program that calculates the minimum cost to travel from the far-left city to the far-right city, given the fuel prices at each city's gas station and the lengths of the roads connecting each city.

Input

Standard input provides the following information. The first line contains an integer $N$ ($2 \le N \le 100\,000$), which represents the number of cities. The next line contains $N-1$ natural numbers, representing the lengths of the roads connecting two adjacent cities from the leftmost road. The following line contains $N$ natural numbers, representing the per liter fuel prices at the gas stations from the leftmost city in order. The distance from the leftmost to the rightmost city is a natural number between 1 and 1,000,000,000. The price per liter is a natural number between 1 and 1,000,000,000.

Output

Standard output should print the minimum cost of traveling from the leftmost city to the rightmost city.
