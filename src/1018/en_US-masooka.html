Repainting the Chessboard

Statement

Jim found a board in his mansion that is divided into $MN$ unit squares, making up an $M \times N$ board. Some squares are painted black, while the rest are painted white. Jim wants to cut this board to make an $8 \times 8$ chessboard.

A chessboard must have alternating black and white squares. Specifically, each square is either black or white, and two squares sharing a side must be of different colors. Therefore, following this definition, there are only two ways to paint a chessboard: one with the top-left square white and the other with it black.

Since there is no guarantee that the board is already painted like a chessboard, Jim thinks he needs to repaint some squares after cutting it into an $8 \times 8$ chessboard. Naturally, he can choose any $8 \times 8$ area. Write a program to determine the minimum number of squares Jim needs to repaint.

Input

The first line contains $N$ and $M$. $N$ and $M$ are natural numbers greater than or equal to 8 and less than or equal to 50. From the second line onwards, the state of each row of the board is given. B represents black, and W represents white.

Output

On the first line, output the minimum number of squares that Jim needs to repaint.
