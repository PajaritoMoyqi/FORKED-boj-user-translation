Average

Statement

Sejun failed his final exam and decided to manipulate his scores before taking them home. First, he chose the highest score among his grades, denoting this value as $M$. Then, he adjusted all his scores using the formula $\text{score}/M \times 100$.

For example, if Sejun's highest score was 70 and his math score was 50, then his math score is recalculated as $50/70 \times 100$, resulting in 71.43.

Write a program that calculates Sejun's new average score using the method described above.

Input

The first line contains the number of subjects $N$ that Sejun has taken in the exam. This value is less than or equal to 1000. The second line gives Sejun's current grades for these subjects. These are non-negative integers no greater than 100, and at least one of these values is greater than 0.

Output

Print the new average on the first line. The answer is considered correct if the absolute or relative error of the output is less than or equal to $10^{-2}$.

Allowing an error of less than $10^{-2}$ does not mean to print precisely up to the second decimal place.
